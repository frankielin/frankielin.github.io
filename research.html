This Just In: Unemployment Surprises and Their Transmission Through the TV News
with Christopher Tice-Raskin
[+abstract]
This paper examines how local exposure to economic news on television influences labor market outcomes in U.S. media markets. To do this, we construct a novel measure of exposure to unemployment news by interacting market‑level television viewership shares with national unemployment forecast errors. We address potential endogeneity in television viewing patterns by using lagged viewership shares, leveraging the high persistence of television viewership rates. The results indicate that higher exposure to news of higher‑than‑expected unemployment reduces local unemployment rates. Additional analysis shows that greater exposure also reduces job destruction, increases separations, and raises hiring rates, consistent with models where workers with higher unemployment expectations engage in increased search intensity.
